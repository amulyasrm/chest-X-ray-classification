The Training Journey

1. Preparing the Data 🥗

Just like an athlete needs good food, an AI needs clean data. We spend a lot of time "preparing" the images.

Augmentation: Training with Variations

If you only see people standing up, you might not recognize someone lying down. "Augmentation" means we take our X-ray images and randomly rotate them, flip them sideways, or change their brightness.

This trick helps the AI become "robust"—it learns to recognize a disease no matter how the patient was positioned when the X-ray was taken.

2. The Learning Rule 🎓

How does the AI actually "learn" from its mistakes?

The Loss Function 📉

This is the AI's "Internal Scorecard." When the AI makes a wrong guess, the Loss Function gives it a high penalty score. The AI's goal is to make this score as low as possible.

The Optimizer 🔧

Think of the Optimizer as a teacher. It looks at the penalty score and tells the AI precisely which "brain cells" (weights) need to change to do better next time. We use a teacher called AdamW.
