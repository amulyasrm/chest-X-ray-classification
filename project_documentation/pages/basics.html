How does AI "See" Diseases?

1. The Neural Network 🧠

A Neural Network is like a digital brain. It is made of many layers. Imagine a team of people sitting in a row. The first person looks at the image and spots simple stuff (lines). They tell the next person, who looks for shapes (circles). This continues until the last person says, "Oh, that shape looks like a lung disease!"

2. Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) 🔍

CNNs are the "Eyes" of AI. They are great at finding local textures.

Imagine moving a magnifying glass over a picture. The glass (or Filter) looks for specific patterns (like edges or dots). The map of everywhere it found those patterns is called a Feature Map.

Filters: Small boxes that scan for specific details.
Convolution: The act of scanning the image with these filters.

3. Transformers & Attention ✨

Transformers are a newer type of AI brain. While CNNs look through a magnifying glass, Transformers look at the Whole Picture at once.

The Power of Attention

"Attention" is precisely what it sounds like. It's like a Flashlight.

If you are looking at a photo of a person in a forest, your brain uses "Attention" to focus on the person and ignore the trees. In our project, the AI uses "Attention" to shine a light on the lungs and ignore the patient's shoulders or the background.

4. Why Combine Them? (The Hybrid) 🤝

If you only use a CNN, you might see the details but miss the big picture. If you only use a Transformer, you might see the big picture but miss the tiny details.

We combined them! The CNN finds the fine textures of the disease, and the Transformer understands where the disease is related to the rest of the body.
