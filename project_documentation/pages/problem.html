The Problem Statement

What is a Chest X-ray? 🦴

An X-ray is like a photograph of the inside of your body. When X-ray beams pass through you, they show bones as white (because they are thick) and lungs as black (because they are full of air).

Why is it hard to detect diseases?

Diseases usually look like "shadows" or "fuzziness" in the lungs.

1. Visual Similarity: Many different diseases look almost exactly the same (a white patch could be Pneumonia or it could be Cancer).
2. Subtle Features: Some diseases are tiny (like a Nodule) and can be easily missed.
3. Fatigue: After looking at 100 X-rays, even the best doctor's eyes can get tired.

It's like playing "Where's Waldo," but Waldo is a transparent ghost hiding in a foggy city.

The Secret Problem: Class Imbalance ⚖️

Imagine trying to learn about animals, but for every 100 cats you see, you only see 1 tiger. You'll become an expert at cats but very bad at finding tigers!

This is Class Imbalance. In our dataset, "No Finding" (healthy) images are very common, but rare diseases like "Hernia" are very hard to find. We have to design our AI specifically to pay attention to these rare "tigers."
