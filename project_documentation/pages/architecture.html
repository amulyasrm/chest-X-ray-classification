Inside the Hybrid Brain

The Workflow 🧬

Here is exactly what happens once you upload an X-ray image to our system:

graph TD A[Start: Raw X-ray] --> B[1. CLAHE: Brightness Check] B --> C[2. Resize: High Resolution] C --> D1[3A. CNN: Texture Expert] C --> D2[3B. Swin ViT: Global Expert] D1 --> E[4. Attention: Focus Filter] E --> F[5. Fusion: Combining Experts] D2 --> F F --> G[End: Disease Prediction]

Step-by-Step Components

1. CLAHE Preprocessing 🔦

What it is: A digital "Contrast" filter.
Why we use it: Some X-rays are dark or blurry. CLAHE makes the faint details (like small shadows) stand out clearly so the AI doesn't miss them.

2. DenseNet CNN 🧬

What it is: A very deep traditional AI eye.
Why we use it: It's been proven for years to be the best at finding patterns in medical images. We use it to extract "local" features.

3. Swin Transformer 🌐

What it is: A modern Transformer that looks at the whole image.
Why we use it: It understands "Context." For example, it knows where the heart is versus the lungs, helping it categorize diseases more scientifically.

4. Spatial Attention 🔦

What it is: A digital flashlight that points to lung regions.
Why we use it: It tells the CNN: "Hey, focus on the lungs, ignore the background noise!" This makes the AI much more accurate.

Feature Fusion: The Final Verdict 🤝

Think of Feature Fusion as a consultation between two expert doctors.

Doctor A (CNN) says: "I see a very specific rough texture here."
Doctor B (Transformer) says: "Based on the whole lung shape, that texture is likely an infection."

By fusing (connecting) their opinions, our model makes a final, smarter decision on whether a disease is present.
