Core Preprocessing Pipeline

Before images reach the model, they undergo a specialized medical imaging enhancement pipeline to normalize lighting and contrast across various X-ray machines.

Phase 1: Baseline (CheXNet 224)

The benchmark model. Follows the original Stanford CheXNet research, utilizing a standard DenseNet121 encoder. It relies on standard imaging features with minimal pixel-level enhancement.

  • Preprocessing: Standard Bilinear Interpolation (224px).
  • Normalization: Zero-mean unit-variance (ImageNet).
  • Parameters: ~7M Weights.
  • Limitation: Susceptible to low-contrast signal loss in raw scans.

Hybrid Deep Dive: CNN-Transformer Fusion

The core novelty of our research. A parallel-stream architecture that captures both Texture (CNN) and Context (Transformer).

Phase 4: Optimization Strategy

Training Hyperparameters

Optimizer AdamW (Decoupled Weight Decay)
Learning Rate 1e-4
Scheduler Cosine Annealing (Cycle=10)
Batch Size 8 (Optimized for 384px)
Epochs 10 (Early Stopped)

Experimental Logic

Phase 4 optimized the Data Integrity layer. We discovered that standard random splitting was causing 'Patient Leakage'. The new pipeline uses mandatory Patient-wise splitting, ensuring the model generalizes to new patients rather than memorizing individual X-ray machine artifacts.
